# Kopi Nangkau Business Analysis

## 1. Business Problem
As its customer base and product portfolio expanded, the company sought to leverage sales data to improve business performance. This project analyzes daily transaction data from 2021–2023 to identify top-performing products, sales trends, seasonal patterns, and growth opportunities for future business strategy.

- **Dataset:** A dataset containing over 6,600 Kopi Nangkau transactions from 2021 to 2023, including transaction date, month, year, units sold, and unit price.
- **Tools:** Phyton, Google Colab

## 3. Key Findings
1. Americano was the best-selling product in 2021, while Cappuccino became the top-selling product in 2022 and 2023.
2. Donut and Croissant were consistently the lowest-selling products.
3. Customers showed a stronger preference for food products than beverages.
4. Latte maintained the most consistent sales performance across all three years.
5. Cappuccino experienced the highest year-over-year sales growth.
6. The company's annual revenue increased consistently throughout the analysis period.
7. Despite its relatively higher price, Cappuccino continued to grow in sales, indicating low price sensitivity among customers.
8. Although Donut and Croissant were among the lowest-priced products, their sales remained weak, suggesting that price was not the primary purchasing factor.
9. Annual price increases were associated with a decline in total units sold, indicating that higher prices negatively affected purchase volume.
10. Latte contributed the highest share of total revenue, while Donut contributed the least.
11. March recorded the highest sales, whereas February and September consistently had the lowest sales.

## 4. Business Recommendations
1. Optimize the product portfolio by replacing low-performing products such as Donut and Croissant with menu items that better complement coffee, such as sandwiches, toast, or cakes.
2. Invest in Cappuccino by expanding its product variants and increasing promotional efforts, as strong demand persists despite its premium price.
3. Leverage peak seasons by launching new products and implementing price adjustments during March and October, when customer demand is consistently high.
4. Boost off-season sales by offering targeted discounts and promotional campaigns during February and September to stimulate customer demand.
